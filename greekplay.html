The Seven Plays in English Verse by Sophocles
OED.

Daughter, what must I think, or do?

ANT.

My sire! We must conform us to the people's will, yielding ere they compel.

OED.

_Reaching out his hand._ Give me thy hand.

ANT.

Thou hast it.

OED.

Strangers, let me not be wronged, when I have trusted you and come from where I stood.

CH.

Assure thee, from this seat no man shall drag thee off against thy will.

OED.

_Nervously._Farther?

CH.

Advance thy foot.

OED.

Yet more?

CH.

Assist him onward, Maiden, thou hast thy sight.
